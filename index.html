About Me

I’m a first-generation college student from Los Angeles, a math teacher in Boston Public Schools, and a builder of AI tools for education. My career sits at the intersection of equity, technology, and classroom practice.

Over nine years at Margarita Muñiz Academy, I have taught mathematics to Students with Limited or Interrupted Formal Education (SLIFE) — and built tools to make that work more scalable and equitable.

I am completing a second Master’s at the Harvard Graduate School of Education, following degrees from Bowdoin College and UMass Boston. My research focuses on AI, mathematics education, and equity for Latinx and multilingual learners.

AI Tool Custom GPT Math Education

EduCan Math Coach

Built for middle school multilingual learners (SLIFE) and math teachers

The Challenge

Students with limited formal education couldn’t access grade-level math tasks. Teachers were spending hours manually scaffolding every lesson — and the results were inconsistent.

How I Solved It

I designed a Custom GPT that generates scaffolded problems, leveled explanations, and language supports — all aligned to Common Core standards — in seconds.

What I Invented

An “Access Layers” framework: each problem ships with a visual anchor, a language bridge, and a grade-level version so every student has an entry point.

What I Learned

Scaffolding works best when invisible to the student. This insight led me to build the Level Up AI Tool — moving from scaffolded problems to scaffolded texts.

📄 Scaffolded problem generation 🌐 Language supports 📊 Standards-aligned outputs ⚡ Reduced teacher planning time

Full-Stack App React Accessibility

Level Up AI Tool

Built for middle and high school teachers supporting ELLs and multilingual learners

The Challenge

Complex texts were inaccessible to ELL students, but differentiating them by hand — at three reading levels, in two languages — was unsustainable for any classroom teacher.

How I Solved It

Built a full-stack app using Lovable and Google Gemini that instantly transforms any text into three Lexile-calibrated reading levels with Spanish translations and scaffolding supports.

What I Invented

A “Fidelity Check” layer: the AI verifies its own output preserves the original main idea, key facts, and sequence before returning results to the teacher.

What I Learned

Accessibility is not just about content level — it’s about rendering. Adding OpenDyslexic font mode and high-contrast themes significantly improved usability for struggling readers.

📖 3 Lexile-calibrated levels 🇪🇸 Spanish translations ✅ AI Fidelity Check ♿ Dyslexia-friendly mode 📤 PDF export

Stack: React 19 · Supabase · Google Gemini · Cloudflare Workers · TypeScript

React Web App Claude API Research

AI & Education Daily Brief

Built for educators who need fast access to peer-reviewed AI and K–12 research

The Challenge

The AI-in-education research landscape moves fast. Busy teachers have no time to comb through journals — but they’re making curriculum decisions that depend on what the research says.

How I Solved It

Designed a React app powered by Claude (Anthropic) that surfaces current peer-reviewed research with academic citation badges, critical analysis, and practical classroom takeaways.

What I Invented

Per-section refresh — teachers can reload just one topic area without regenerating the entire brief, saving time and reducing friction.

What I Learned

Teachers trust tools that show their work. Citation badges became the most-mentioned feature in feedback — they signal credibility and academic rigor.

📚 Academic citation badges 🔄 Per-section refresh 🖨 Print-to-PDF 💾 Persistent storage

React Web App Claude API Professional Learning

RStudio for Educators

Built for teachers learning data analysis without adding to their workload

The Challenge

Data literacy is essential for teachers — but a traditional graduate course on R was adding to burnout, not building capacity. The learning experience needed to feel like a tool, not a course.

How I Solved It

Built a differentiated, self-paced React app that guides teachers through RStudio at their own level — with tiers based on prior knowledge and built-in practice and assessment cycles.

What I Invented

A “learn, practice, assess” loop embedded in each tier so mastery is confirmed before moving forward, rather than progressing linearly through content.

What I Learned

Meeting teachers where they are — in knowledge and in time — is itself a design principle. Tiered entry is the difference between a tool people use and one they abandon.

🎚 Knowledge-based tiers 🔁 Learn → Practice → Assess loop 👩‍🏫 Teacher-differentiated UX

React Web App Claude API Instructional Coaching

Core Moves Math Teacher Tracker

Built for math coaches and teachers who want data on student-centered teaching moves

The Challenge

In most math classrooms, the teacher does the heavy cognitive lifting. Coaches needed a fast, reliable way to track whether teaching moves were actually eliciting student thinking across multiple sessions.

How I Solved It

Built a tap-friendly React app that records teaching moves in real time during observations, stores multi-session data, and generates a dashboard to support coaching conversations.

What I Invented

Tap-to-log input designed for one-handed use during live classroom observation — because a tool that interrupts the observer simply does not get used.

What I Learned

The dashboard revealed patterns across sessions that neither coach nor teacher had noticed in the moment — turning observation data into a shared coaching narrative.

👆 Tap-to-log input 📊 Multi-session dashboard 🎯 Individualized goal tracking 🤖 AI coaching feedback
